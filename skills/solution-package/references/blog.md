# Blog Post Guide

## Purpose

Extends solution narrative for **external distribution and SEO**. Adds external credibility with authoritative sources.

## Critical Rule: External Sources Only

**Never use demo-specific metrics!**

### Acceptable Sources

| Source Type | Examples |
|-------------|----------|
| Industry analysts | Gartner, McKinsey, Deloitte |
| Trade associations | Industry bodies, professional orgs |
| Academic research | Peer-reviewed studies |
| Government | Regulatory statistics |

### Attribution Pattern

> According to [source], [audience] with [input metric] can expect [output metric].

## Structure

### Title
- Quantifiable promise
- SEO keywords
- Under 60 characters

### Executive Summary (BLUF)
- First paragraph = entire message
- Who, what, outcome

### Problem/Stakes
- Industry context
- Cost of inaction
- External statistics

### Solution
- High-level approach
- Snowflake differentiation
- Technical depth (appropriate level)

### Results/Evidence
- External benchmarks
- Methodology stated
- Assumptions documented

### CTA
- Offer value, not sales pitch
- Clear next step
- Working link

## Length Selection

| Length | Words | Use Case |
|--------|-------|----------|
| Short | 800-1,200 | Single concept, tactical tip |
| Medium | 1,500-2,000 | Method explanation, case study |
| Long | 2,400+ | Comprehensive guide, thought leadership |

## Quality Checkpoint

- [ ] No demo-specific metrics used
- [ ] All statistics have documented sources
- [ ] Title is quantifiable promise
- [ ] First paragraph is executive summary (BLUF)
- [ ] CTA offers value, not sales pitch
